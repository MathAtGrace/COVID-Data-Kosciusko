Confirmed Deaths from COVID19

Deaths data are the best indicator of the real prevalence of the disease, but deaths data lags behind case data.

All numbers in this graph are per ten thousand. Dots are the number of new deaths recorded each day. Dashed lines are a weighted average.

Confirmed Cases of COVID19

The case data below is also per ten thousand. Dots are new confirmed cases of COVID19 each day. The dashed lines are weighted averages.

Positive Test Rate

Looking at the percentage of tests that come back positive can signal which states might have low case numbers because of lack of testing.

For example, Michigan had a much higher death rate per capita than Illinois in mid-April, even though their case numbers per capita were only a little higher in late March and early April.
